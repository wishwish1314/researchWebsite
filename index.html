I am a human-AI interaction designer and researcher. My research goal is to better understand how to bring human-centered mindset into AI technologies and how to make them accessible for human-centered AI product innovation. Recently, my research is focused on how to build user-centered AI-driven application in smart homes.

At the intersection of social science, psychology and computer science, Human and AI interaction, and human and algorithm interaction, I am exploring how cutting-edge AI systems are developed and appropriated/anticipated by users in the context of smart homes and the ongoing attempts to save energy and respond to climate change. Due to the evolving nature of AI systems, this includes challenges such as creating meaningful interfaces and feedback for the end-users, as well as making efficient use of systems whose behaviour adapts and evolves in response and collaboration with the inhabitants of smart homes over longer periods of time. Based on studies in AI companies and research institutions as well as on studies with users of current smart home technologies, I will investigate different design methodologies with regard to their challenges and potentials for building novel AI products that help users to save energy without giving up control over their lives.
